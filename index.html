Logger Tool

This page began as an experiment for a course I took that focused on gathering data for User Experience design. We partook in an ethnography study, which required us to choose a location and log everything that took place. These included people walking into our environment, someone answering a phone call, or even spilling their drink. The point of our experiment was to notice any patterns within our locations, and unfortunately the best way to keep track of everything, was using a pen and paper. I wrote this simple app to replace writing events down manually, and also add the time and date into each log entry.

However, this doesn't limit a user to using this tool for just that purpose. You can use this for pretty much anything. Some examples include:

  • Keeping track of your diet
  • Creating a virtual diary
  • Track your sleep schedule
  • And anything you'd find this tool useful for

Log your results

The following form allows you to log your entries. Each entry will contain your written recording and your choice of style of a timestamp. The timestamp may contain each measurement from year, all the way to milliseconds. And you may also swap the format to contain the timestamp first, followed by your entry, or vice versa.
